COS112

Innovation in Artificial Intelligence and Human-Computer Interaction

Computer ethics refers to the moral principles and values that guide the use of computers and technology. It involves considering the impact of technology on individuals, organizations, and society as a whole.

Key Issues in Computer Ethics:

  • Privacy : the right to control personal information and protect it from unauthorized access
  • Security : the protection of computer systems and data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction
  • Intellectual Property : the rights of creators and owners of digital content, such as software, music, and videos
  • Cyberbullying : the use of technology to harass, intimidate, or harm others
  • Digital Divide : the gap between those who have access to technology and those who do not

Ethical Theories and Principles:

  • Utilitarianism : the idea that actions are right if they promote the greatest happiness for the greatest number
  • Deontology : the idea that actions are right if they conform to a set of moral rules and duties
  • Virtue Ethics : the idea that actions are right if they reflect the character and moral virtues of the individual
  • Respect for Autonomy : the principle of respecting the independence and decision-making capacity of individuals
  • Non-Maleficence : the principle of doing no harm to others

Best Practices for Computer Ethics:

  • Use strong passwords and keep them confidential
  • Use antivirus software and keep it up to date
  • Be cautious when downloading software and files from the internet
  • Respect the intellectual property rights of others
  • Report any incidents of cyberbullying or harassment

Examples:

  • A company collects personal data from its customers without their consent. Is this a violation of privacy?
  • A student downloads copyrighted music from the internet without paying for it. Is this a violation of intellectual property rights?

Tips for Applying Computer Ethics:

  • Consider the potential consequences of your actions on others and on society.
  • Respect the rights and dignity of others in the digital world.
  • Stay informed about current issues and best practices in computer ethics.
